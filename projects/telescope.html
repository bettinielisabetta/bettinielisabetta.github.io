Project Overview

My work at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) is part of the development activities for the Habitable Worlds Observatory (HWO), a next-generation space telescope concept aimed at directly imaging and characterizing Earth-like exoplanets.

Within this context, I contribute to IRIS, a ground-based technological testbed designed to support the development and validation of high-precision optical systems for future space observatories.

Technical Contributions

My work focuses on the structural dynamics and high-fidelity modeling of precision opto-mechanical systems. I develop finite element models for dynamic characterization, perform modal analysis, and support experimental validation activities.

This includes correlation between numerical models and experimental data, as well as the development of reduced-order models to support control design and system-level analysis.

The overall objective is to ensure structural stability and dynamic performance compatible with the stringent requirements of high-contrast imaging systems.
